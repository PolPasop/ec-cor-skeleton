    <section class="cor-l-plenary__main">
        <p>Normally chaired by CoR's President, the main purpose of the plenary sessions is to:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Vote on Opinions, reports and resolutions </li>
            <li>Adopt the CoR's political programme at the beginning of every term </li>
            <li>Elect a President, First Vice-president and the remaining members of the Bureau </li>
            <li>Set up policy commissions within the institution </li>
            <li>Approve CoR draft estimates for expenditure and revenue </li>
            <li>Revise and agree on CoR Rules of Procedure</li>
        </ul>

        <p>The relevant CoR commission will appoint one of its Members – known as the rapporteur – to draft an Opinion on the legislative proposal which is then voted on during one of CoR plenaries. If the Opinion receives a majority votes in its favour (simple majority), it is adopted and handed over to the other EU institutions for further scrutiny.</p>

        <p>The CoR may also proactively adopt a position on a particular issue through an own-initiative Opinion.</p>
    </section>
